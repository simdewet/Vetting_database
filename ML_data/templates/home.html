Transient Vetting of MeerLICHT Data on S190814bv
A web interface created by Simon de Wet

On the 14th of August 2019, a GW event was detected by the LIGO/Virgo Collaboration. The event was given the name S190814bv, and was significant for a number of reasons. Firstly, it was incredibly well-localized for a GW event: the 90% probability region had an area of 23 deg2. Secondly, the false alarm rate was extremely low at 1 event per every 1025 years. Lastly, the event was believed to be caused by a neutron star - black hole merger. Observing such a merger would have been a major breakthrough.

MeerLICHT

The MeerLICHT optical telescope in Sutherland performed an intense observing campaign of the GW localization region, observing almost every night for a 10 day period after the event. A total of 16 fields (shown below) encompassed the 90% localization region, with each field being observed up to 3 times in a night, in each of the u, q and i bands. Due to vignetting caused by the dome malfunctioning, a large fraction of the observations had to be excluded from our transient analysis.

Transients

This web interface was designed to allow a user to manually vet transients detected in the usable transient files produced by BlackBOX and ZOGY. Go to the Vetting tab to start vetting transients!
